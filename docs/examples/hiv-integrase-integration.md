# HIV Integrase-Mediated Integration Sequence

> **Color key:** <span style="color:#F6D64A; font-weight:700;">viral DNA</span> | <span style="color:#45A3F3; font-weight:700;">target chromosome</span> | <span style="color:#71C26B; font-weight:700;">integrase enzyme</span> | <span style="color:#E04B4B; font-weight:700;">cleavage sites</span>

1. **<span style="color:#71C26B;">Integrase</span> trims the <span style="color:#F6D64A;">viral DNA</span> 3′ ends.**
   - Removes a few terminal nucleotides to expose reactive **3′-OH** groups on each <span style="color:#F6D64A;">viral</span> end, indicated by <span style="color:#E04B4B;">cleavage</span> marks.

2. **Staggered nicking of the <span style="color:#45A3F3;">target chromosome</span>.**
   - <span style="color:#71C26B;">Integrase</span> creates offset cuts in the <span style="color:#45A3F3;">host DNA</span>, leaving short single-stranded overhangs (the <span style="color:#E04B4B;">staggered nicks</span>).

3. **Strand transfer: joining <span style="color:#F6D64A;">viral</span> 3′ ends to <span style="color:#45A3F3;">host</span> DNA.**
   - Each exposed **3′-OH** on the <span style="color:#F6D64A;">viral DNA</span> attacks a phosphodiester bond in the nicked <span style="color:#45A3F3;">target DNA</span>, forming covalent links. This is the <strong>integrase-catalyzed strand-transfer</strong> step.

4. **Gap filling and repair.**
   - Host polymerases fill remaining gaps and ligases seal residual nicks, completing the insertion.

5. **Outcome: integrated provirus flanked by short direct repeats.**
   - Because the <span style="color:#45A3F3;">host</span> cuts are staggered, repair duplicates the target-site sequence, leaving the integrated <span style="color:#F6D64A;">viral DNA</span> bordered by short direct repeats of <span style="color:#45A3F3;">host DNA</span>.

---

## High-yield takeaways

- <span style="color:#71C26B;">Integrase</span> both processes 3′ ends and performs strand transfer.
- The chemistry is a **transesterification**: the energy stored in the phosphodiester bond drives the 3′-OH attack without external ATP.
- Target-site duplication creates characteristic short direct repeats flanking the provirus.
